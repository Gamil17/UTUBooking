# UTUBooking Brand Voice — Tone of Voice Reference

## Core Personality
UTUBooking sounds like a knowledgeable travel advisor who has personally
made the pilgrimage and booked thousands of travelers. Warm, helpful, and
expert — not corporate, not salesy, not robotic.

---

## 10 Golden Sentences (UTUBooking voice at its best)

Use these as a writing baseline. New content should feel like these.

1. "Finding a hotel 150 metres from the Haram gate isn't luck — it's knowing where to look."
2. "Book Ramadan Umrah at least four months ahead. The good rooms go first, every single year."
3. "That SAR 450-per-night room looks cheap until you factor in the 40-minute bus ride to Fajr."
4. "UTUBooking measures walking time from the hotel lobby to the gate — not from the hotel pin on a map."
5. "Hajj packages fill by February. In March, you're choosing from what's left."
6. "A halal-certified kitchen isn't a tag the hotel adds — we verify the prayer mats, the qibla markers, and the kitchen certification."
7. "You don't need a travel agent. You need the right information at the right time."
8. "The proximity filter was built for one reason: so your grandmother doesn't have to walk 800 metres at Fajr."
9. "Three stars near the Haram beats five stars in Aziziyah — every time, for every pilgrim."
10. "We built UTUBooking for Arabic speakers, not for Arabic translation."

## Opinion Starters (for bold, direct paragraphs)

Start opinionated paragraphs with one of these:
- "Look," — then the direct truth
- "Here's the reality:" — then the honest assessment
- "The short answer:" — for direct answers to common questions
- "Most [X] guides won't tell you this:" — for contrarian insight
- "Let's be honest:" — for acknowledging the hard parts of pilgrimage travel

End sections with forward-looking hooks, not summaries:
- ✅ "The next section shows you exactly how to use this to your advantage."
- ✅ "Below, we walk through the specific filters that make the difference."
- ❌ "In summary, UTUBooking is a great tool for Umrah booking."

## Tone Attributes

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** | "Planning your Umrah is one of the most meaningful journeys of your life" | "UTUBooking provides Umrah booking services" |
| **Expert** | Cite real distances, real prices, real booking windows | Vague claims without specifics |
| **Active** | "UTUBooking finds you the closest hotel" | "Hotels are found by UTUBooking" |
| **Simple** | 8th-grade reading level, short sentences | Jargon, corporate buzzwords |
| **Respectful** | "pilgrims", "worshippers", "the holy cities" | "tourists" for Makkah/Madinah visitors |

## Sentence & Paragraph Rules
- One idea per paragraph. Maximum 2-4 sentences.
- Contractions encouraged: you'll, it's, don't, we've
- Active voice always
- No em dashes. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or split into two sentences.
- Short words over long: "use" not "utilise", "start" not "commence"

## What We Never Say
- "world-class" / "best-in-class" / "cutting-edge" (overused)
- "seamless" / "hassle-free" (every travel company says this)
- "leverage" / "synergy" / "ecosystem" (corporate jargon)
- Competitor names in negative context (legal risk)
- "tourist" when referring to pilgrims visiting Makkah or Madinah

## Currency & Pricing Voice
- Always show SAR first for KSA/Gulf audience
- Format: "from SAR 850 per night" not "SAR850/night"
- When showing price ranges: "SAR 400 to SAR 1,800 per night"
- Seasonal context always: "prices increase 200-400% during Hajj season"

## Islamic Sensitivity
- Use "Masjid Al-Haram" not just "the mosque"
- "Prophet's Mosque" or "Masjid Al-Nabawi" for Madinah
- "Hajj" and "Umrah" always capitalised
- Close with spiritual acknowledgment in pilgrimage content:
  "May Allah accept your Umrah" / "May your journey be blessed"
- Never be flippant about the religious significance of the journey

## Arabic Content Voice
- Headings: Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha) — formal register
- Body text: Gulf dialect acceptable — closer to how people actually search
- Answer Capsules: Gulf dialect preferred (matches voice search patterns)
- Flag all Arabic content with [REVIEW: native speaker required]

## Sample Brand Voice — Good vs Bad

**Topic:** Hajj hotel booking

**Bad (corporate):**
"UTUBooking offers a comprehensive portfolio of accommodation solutions
in close proximity to the Masjid Al-Haram complex, optimised for the
discerning pilgrim seeking a seamless travel experience."

**Good (UTUBooking voice):**
"Finding a hotel close to Masjid Al-Haram can make or break your Hajj
experience. Every step counts when you're performing Tawaf multiple times
a day in the Makkah heat. UTUBooking's Haram proximity filter shows you
the exact walking time from any hotel to the nearest gate — so you can
choose with confidence, not guesswork."
