## Phase 6 HR Jurisdictions (Added Q1 2028)

### Pakistan
Labour law: Pakistan Labour Laws 1934 (updated). Key rules:
- End of Service Gratuity: 1 month salary per year for employees > 3 years
- Working hours: 48/week max, overtime at 150%
- All contracts bilingual: English + Urdu

### India
Labour law: Indian Labour Codes (consolidated 2020). Key rules:
- Gratuity after 5 years continuous service
- PF (Provident Fund): 12% employer + 12% employee contribution
- All job postings must comply with non-discrimination laws
- Contracts available in Hindi upon employee request

## Phase 8 — European HR Jurisdictions (Added Q1 2027)

### United Kingdom (GB)
Labour law: UK Employment Rights Act 1996 + Equality Act 2010 + Working Time Regulations 1998
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: National Living Wage (£11.44/hr for 21+, reviewed annually April)
- Statutory notice: 1 week per year of service (up to 12 weeks max)
- Statutory sick pay: £116.75/week up to 28 weeks
- Redundancy: statutory redundancy pay after 2+ years service (1.5 weeks pay per year for 41+)
- Annual leave: 28 days minimum (including 8 bank holidays) for full-time employees
- Right to work: verify using Home Office online service before employment starts (post-Brexit requirement)
- Contracts: written statement of employment particulars required from Day 1
- GDPR: UK GDPR applies to all employee personal data — data minimisation, retention policy required
- Protected characteristics: 9 protected characteristics under Equality Act — NEVER ask about age, religion, pregnancy, sexuality in interviews
- All UK offer letters require legal review before sending

### Germany (DE)
Labour law: German Civil Code (BGB) + Works Constitution Act (BetrVG) + Minimum Wage Act (MiLoG)
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: €12.41/hr (2024), reviewed annually
- Probation period: max 6 months
- Notice periods: 4 weeks minimum; up to 7 months for long tenure (scales with years of service)
- Annual leave: 24 working days minimum (typically 28–30 in tech sector)
- Works council (Betriebsrat): mandatory for companies with 5+ permanent employees — consultation required for hiring/dismissal/restructuring
- Overtime: generally not compensated in cash — must be taken as time off
- Contracts: must be in German (dual-language acceptable but German governs)
- Data: employee data subject to DSGVO (German GDPR implementation) + Bundesdatenschutzgesetz
- Note: German labour law strongly protects employees — wrongful dismissal claims are common; always consult German employment lawyer before terminating

### France (FR)
Labour law: French Labour Code (Code du travail) + industry-specific collective agreements (conventions collectives)
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: SMIC — €11.65/hr gross (2024)
- Working hours: 35-hour legal week; overtime requires works council agreement for regular extra hours
- Annual leave: 5 weeks (25 working days) minimum
- Probation: 1–4 months depending on contract type and collective agreement
- Redundancy: complex procedure — economic dismissal requires works council consultation + state notification (DIRECCTE)
- Health insurance: employer contributes ~13% of salary to social security (cotisations patronales)
- Notice: 1–3 months depending on seniority and collective agreement
- All French job postings must use gender-neutral language (H/F/X notation) and French language (Loi Toubon)

### Netherlands (NL)
Labour law: Dutch Civil Code Book 7 + Work and Security Act (WWZ)
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: €13.27/hr (2024)
- Probation: max 2 months for contracts > 6 months
- Annual leave: 20 working days minimum (4× weekly hours)
- Dismissal: requires approval from UWV (Employee Insurance Agency) or court — cannot terminate at-will
- Temporary contracts: max 3 consecutive fixed-term contracts within 3 years, then permanent
- Transition payment: payable from Day 1 on dismissal (1/3 monthly salary per year of service)
- 30% ruling: highly skilled migrants may qualify for 30% tax-free ruling — mention in expat offers

### Poland (PL)
Labour law: Polish Labour Code (Kodeks pracy)
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: PLN 4,242/month (2024), increasing to PLN 4,300 in mid-2024
- Annual leave: 20 days (< 10 years service), 26 days (10+ years)
- Notice: 2 weeks – 3 months depending on contract length
- Probation: max 3 months
- Social insurance (ZUS): employer contributes ~20% of salary

### Switzerland (CH)
Labour law: Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) + cantonal employment regulations
Key rules:
- Minimum wage: varies by canton (Geneva CHF 24.32/hr, Zürich no federal minimum but industry agreements apply)
- Annual leave: 4 weeks minimum (5 weeks for under 20)
- Notice: 1 month (Year 1), 2 months (Years 2–9), 3 months (Year 10+) — standard; can be extended by contract
- AHV/IV/EO (social security): 5.3% employer + 5.3% employee
- BVG (occupational pension): employer must match employee contribution (varies by age)
- Contracts: can be in German, French, or Italian depending on canton; English acceptable for international hires if both parties agree
- Work permits: B permit (1-year renewable) for EU/EFTA nationals; L permit (short-stay); always verify cantonal requirements

## EU-Wide HR Data Rules (GDPR)
- Employee personal data is PERSONAL DATA under GDPR — full GDPR obligations apply
- Retention: keep employment records for statutory minimum (UK: 6 years post-employment; DE: 10 years for payroll)
- Background checks: reference checks permitted; criminal record checks restricted (only if role-specific justification)
- Monitoring: employee monitoring (email, software) requires works council agreement (DE) or employee notice (UK/FR/NL)
- Right of access: EU employees can request a copy of their HR data — respond within 30 days
- Never store EU employee data on non-EU servers without Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- All EU employment contracts require legal review before sending
