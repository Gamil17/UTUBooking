## SLIDE 2 — The Problem

**Headline:** 1.5 Billion Muslims Travel With Tools Built for Non-Muslim Journeys

- **Fragmentation is severe.** Muslim travellers cross at least 6–8 digital touchpoints to complete a single Hajj or Umrah booking: government portals, airline sites, hotel aggregators, visa apps, Mehram verification services, and currency exchange platforms — none of which talk to each other.
- **Halal requirements are invisible to mainstream OTAs.** Booking.com, Expedia, and Agoda do not surface halal food certification, proximity to Haram, gender-segregated facilities, or prayer-time amenities. Every filter that matters to a Muslim traveller must be applied manually — if it exists at all.
- **National pilgrimage quotas are opaque.** Turkey, Indonesia, and Pakistan each have multi-year waitlists for Hajj permits. No commercial platform surfaces this data. Families plan — and spend — without knowing their actual departure window.
- **Payment infrastructure is mismatched.** 40% of Muslim-majority markets are underbanked. STC Pay, JazzCash, Easypaisa, iyzico, and Tabung Haji savings withdrawal are the real payment rails — yet every mainstream OTA routes these users to Stripe or Visa-only checkouts.
- **The result:** Muslim travellers convert at half the rate of comparable Western travellers on global OTAs, not because of lack of intent — but because of friction designed into the product.

> **Speaker note:** "The $220 billion Muslim travel market is growing at 8% CAGR, yet the infrastructure serving it is the same generic stack built for a Vegas vacation. That's the gap we exist to close."
