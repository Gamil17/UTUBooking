I started UTUBooking because I watched my father book Umrah the same way his father did: calling an agent, waiting for a WhatsApp confirmation, and hoping the hotel was real when he landed in Jeddah.

That experience — that friction, that uncertainty, that lack of dignity — is not a small inconvenience. It is the norm for millions of Muslim families booking the most important trip of their lives.

Today, I am proud to share that UTUBooking is Series A ready.

We have built the first Arabic-native, mobile-first SaaS platform purpose-built for Hajj and Umrah travel. One app — search, book, pay via STC Pay or Mada, and manage your sacred journey end to end. For travel operators, a white-label B2B engine that puts our infrastructure inside their brand. Seven production microservices running on AWS ECS in Bahrain, load-tested to 100,000 concurrent users at Hajj peak.

We are live in KSA and UAE. We are onboarding our first B2B partners in Jordan. Morocco and Tunisia are next.

The Hajj and Umrah market is a USD 20B+ annual opportunity, and it is still largely offline. Vision 2030 targets 30 million Umrah visitors per year by 2030. The tailwind is structural, the demand is faith-driven, and the technology gap is enormous.

We are raising USD 5M to close that gap — and to make sure every Muslim pilgrim, regardless of where they live, can plan their journey with the dignity and transparency they deserve.
