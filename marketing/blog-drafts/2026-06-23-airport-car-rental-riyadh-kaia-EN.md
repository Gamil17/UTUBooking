# Car Rental at Riyadh King Khalid International Airport (KAIA): The Complete 2026 Guide

Riyadh moves fast. Business meetings on one side of the city, government offices on the other, and a stretch of highway connecting it all that taxis and ride-hailing apps can make frustrating at peak hours. For most travelers arriving at King Khalid International Airport, renting a car is the practical choice.

The airport sits roughly 35 km north of central Riyadh. Public transit covers the route, but Riyadh's scale means a car gives you real flexibility. Whether you're here for a three-day business trip or planning a road trip down to Jeddah, picking up a rental at KAIA puts you in control from the moment you land.

This guide covers everything: where to collect your car, which brands operate at the airport, what rates look like in 2026, and what to read before signing.

## Where do I pick up a rental car at King Khalid Airport Riyadh?

Car rental counters at KAIA are located in Terminals 1 and 5. Most international arrivals process through Terminal 5, where you'll find a dedicated rental row in the arrivals hall. Terminal 1 handles domestic routes and some regional carriers, with its own counter area past baggage claim.

After clearing immigration and collecting your bags, follow the ground transportation signs toward the arrivals exit. The rental counters are positioned before you reach the main exit doors, on your left in Terminal 5. You don't need to leave the terminal building or take a shuttle in most cases.

A few providers operate vehicle lots off-airport and offer a short shuttle transfer instead of an in-terminal desk. When you book, check whether your provider offers direct counter pickup or a shuttle. In-terminal counters typically get you moving faster, which matters if you've just come off a long flight.

Counter hours vary by provider. Most open from 06:00 to midnight daily. If your flight arrives after midnight, confirm operating hours when you book, or look for a provider that offers 24-hour pickup. UTUBooking displays pickup hour information on each car listing so you know before you confirm.

The main brands at KAIA in 2026 are Budget, Hertz, Europcar, Lumi, Yelo, and Theeb. Each covers a different part of the market.

## How much does airport car rental cost at Riyadh in 2026?

Daily rates at KAIA typically range from SAR 130 to SAR 250 for economy cars, SAR 280 to SAR 500 for mid-size and full-size sedans, and SAR 450 to SAR 900 or more for SUVs and premium vehicles. Luxury and full-size SUV categories can exceed SAR 1,200 per day. All figures vary by provider, season, and booking lead time.

Airport pickup locations typically add a concession fee compared to off-airport rates. This is standard across most major airports globally. The airport fee is usually SAR 30 to SAR 80 per rental, not per day. It appears as a line item during checkout.

Insurance is where costs can shift significantly. Basic Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) is often included in the quoted rate, but the excess liability can be high. Reducing the excess to near zero typically costs an additional SAR 40 to SAR 120 per day depending on the provider and vehicle category. Read the insurance terms before you confirm.

Fuel policy also affects the total cost. Most providers in Saudi Arabia use a full-to-full policy: you collect the car with a full tank and return it full. Some use a full-to-empty policy where you pre-pay for fuel. Full-to-full is generally more cost-effective if you plan to use the car heavily.

## Do I need an international driving permit to rent a car in Saudi Arabia?

The answer depends on your nationality and the license you hold. GCC citizens (Saudi, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Qatar) can rent using their national ID and home country license. No international permit is needed.

Travelers from countries whose licenses are printed in Arabic or English can often rent without an International Driving Permit (IDP), but providers vary in how they apply this rule. If your license is in another language, get an IDP from your home country's motoring authority before you travel. It's a simple document to obtain and avoids any complications at the counter.

Residents of Saudi Arabia holding a valid Saudi driving license can rent on that license regardless of nationality.

Citizens of some countries are legally required to obtain a Saudi driving license after a certain period of residency. If you're a long-term resident, check the current GAZT and traffic authority guidelines to confirm what applies to you.

The short answer: GCC nationals, bring your home license. Everyone else, carry your IDP and your original license. Confirm with your rental provider when you book if you're unsure.

## What is the best car category for driving in Riyadh?

An SUV is the most comfortable choice for most trips in and around Riyadh. Highway speeds on the Ring Road and King Fahd Road regularly reach 120 to 140 km/h, and Saudi drivers tend to keep pace. A larger, more stable vehicle handles the highway runs between meetings and airport transfers with less fatigue.

For pure city driving within a specific district, an economy or compact car is cheaper to park and easier to thread through narrower commercial streets in areas like Al-Malaz or Batha. Parking in central Riyadh has improved with paid zones, but spaces are tight. A smaller footprint helps.

If you're driving outside Riyadh, for example toward the Edge of the World (Jabal Tuwaiq), a 4x4 or high-clearance SUV is not optional. Gravel approaches to popular viewpoints are not paved.

For business travel between the airport, hotels in Al Olaya or the Diplomatic Quarter, and meeting rooms in King Abdullah Financial District (KAFD), a standard sedan or mid-size SUV covers the job. Most business travelers choose a mid-size SUV for the combination of road presence and practical fuel consumption.

## How far is King Khalid Airport from central Riyadh?

KAIA is approximately 35 km north of central Riyadh. The drive to hotels in Al Olaya district typically takes 25 to 40 minutes, depending on traffic. Rush hour on the Northern Ring Road can extend that to 60 minutes or more.

The main route from the airport follows Airport Road south toward the King Fahd Road interchange. Toll roads (via the Sayer system) operate on some urban expressways in Riyadh. You'll need to register a payment method or confirm your rental includes toll coverage.

The Riyadh Metro's Green Line (Line 3) reaches the airport via the King Khalid International Airport Station. The metro is clean, reliable, and significantly cheaper than taxis. However, it does not cover the full city and requires connections for many hotel locations. If you're traveling light and staying near a metro station, it's a practical option. If you have luggage and multiple stops planned, the rental car pays for itself on day one.

Ride-hailing apps (Uber and Careem both operate in Riyadh) are available from the airport. A one-way ride to central Riyadh typically costs SAR 60 to SAR 120. For a multi-day stay with daily travel, a rental almost always works out more economical.

## Can I rent a car in Riyadh and return it to another city?

Yes. One-way rentals between Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dammam are available from most major providers at KAIA. One-way fees apply and vary by provider and distance. Riyadh to Jeddah one-way fees typically range from SAR 150 to SAR 350 on top of the daily rate. Riyadh to Dammam fees are generally lower, around SAR 100 to SAR 200.

Book one-way rentals in advance. Fleet availability at drop-off locations can be limited, and last-minute one-way requests often carry higher fees or may not be offered at all.

If you're driving to Makkah or Madinah, note that non-Muslim travelers are not permitted to enter the city centers of either holy city. Muslim travelers with a valid permit can drive in. Confirm road access requirements and any Hajj season road restrictions before planning this route.

## What should I check before signing a car rental agreement in Saudi Arabia?

Walk through five things before you sign anything at the counter.

First, confirm the insurance coverage. Know what your CDW covers, what the excess liability is, and whether a personal accident insurance (PAI) add-on is included or optional. If the counter agent offers an upgrade, ask for the excess figure before you decide.

Second, check the fuel policy. Full-to-full or pre-paid fuel. If it's pre-paid, the per-liter rate is almost always higher than what you'd pay at a petrol station. Decline pre-paid fuel unless you genuinely won't have time to refuel before return.

Third, confirm the mileage allowance. Most Saudi rental agreements include unlimited kilometres for local Riyadh rentals. For one-way or long-term rentals, there may be a daily cap. Exceeding the cap adds a per-kilometre charge that adds up quickly on Riyadh-Jeddah drives.

Fourth, do a full walk-around of the vehicle before you drive off. Note every scratch, dent, and scuff on the condition report. Take photos with your phone and timestamp them. This protects you when you return the car.

Fifth, ask about the toll road policy. Some providers include a transponder with the vehicle. Others charge a handling fee on top of any tolls incurred. Know which applies to your rental before you leave the lot.
