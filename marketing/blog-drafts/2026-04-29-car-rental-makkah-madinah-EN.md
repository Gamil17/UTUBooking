## How far is Makkah to Madinah by road?

The drive from Makkah to Madinah is approximately 420 kilometres via Highway 15, known as the Makkah-Madinah Expressway. Under normal conditions, the journey takes between four and five hours. During peak Hajj and Umrah seasons, traffic near the holy cities can extend that to six or seven hours.

That distance sits comfortably within a single driving day. Most pilgrims find the drive far less stressful than they expect, especially compared to airport transfers and crowded train stations.

Highway 15 is the main artery connecting the two cities. It cuts through the Hejaz mountain region, passing through Al-Jumum and skirting the edge of Taif. The road is well-maintained, dual-carriageway for most of its length, and clearly signposted in both Arabic and English.

Speed limits are enforced by camera. The standard limit on open highway stretches is 120 km/h, dropping to 100 km/h near towns. Saudi traffic cameras are [among the most active in the region](https://www.sauditourism.sa/en/travelling-to-saudi/driving-in-saudi-arabia), and fines are issued to the rental car company, then passed directly to you.

One practical tip: fuel stations are spaced roughly every 50 to 80 kilometres along the route. Fill up before leaving the city limits of Makkah if you are departing in peak hours, because the stations near the Haram area get busy.

## What documents do foreign pilgrims need to drive in Saudi Arabia?

**The short answer:** foreign visitors need a valid passport, an active Saudi visit or Umrah visa, and an International Driving Permit (IDP) issued in their home country alongside their national driving licence. Without the IDP, most rental companies will not hand over the keys.

The IDP must be issued by an authorised body in your country before you travel. In the UK, that is the [AA or RAC](https://www.theaa.com/driving-abroad/international-driving-permit/). In the US, it is the [AAA](https://www.aaa.com/vacation/idpf.html). In most countries, the process takes a few days and costs very little. Do not leave this until you arrive.

Required documents at the rental desk:

- Original passport (not a photocopy)
- Valid Saudi visa (Umrah, Hajj, or tourist visa)
- National driving licence from your home country
- International Driving Permit
- Credit card in the renter's name for the security deposit

Some nationalities, including Gulf Cooperation Council citizens, can drive in Saudi Arabia on their national licence without an IDP. Check [Saudi Arabia's General Directorate of Traffic](https://www.moi.gov.sa/wps/portal/Home/sectors/publicsecurity/traffic/) for the full exemption list.

Pilgrims from Pakistan, Indonesia, India, Malaysia, and most African nations will need the IDP without exception. Apply for yours at least two weeks before travel.

## What is the drive like on Highway 15 (Makkah-Madinah Expressway)?

This section deserves honest description rather than a simple answer, because the drive experience changes significantly depending on when you travel.

During off-peak months, Highway 15 is a genuinely pleasant road journey. The landscape shifts from the dense urban grid of Makkah into open desert within 30 minutes. Rocky hills rise on either side as the road climbs through the Hejaz region. The air is cleaner. The pace slows. Many pilgrims describe the drive as meditative, a time between the two sacred places that carries its own quiet weight.

During Hajj season, the picture changes. The Saudi government [controls road access to Makkah strictly during Hajj](https://www.sauditourism.sa/en/travelling-to-saudi/hajj), and private vehicles face checkpoint delays and route restrictions. If your travel falls during Dhul Hijjah, check current road restrictions before committing to a self-drive plan. Buses and official Hajj transport may be the more reliable option during those specific days.

Umrah season is generally fine for private travel, though the weeks leading into Ramadan and the first days of Ramadan itself see significantly heavier traffic on the expressway.

Road quality is good. The Saudi government has invested heavily in Highway 15 infrastructure over the past decade. Lane markings are clear, and the road is lit in populated sections. Night driving is common and generally safe, though fatigue management matters on any 420-kilometre stretch.

Rest stops with basic food, prayer facilities, and clean bathrooms appear roughly every 80 to 100 kilometres. These are well-run. Plan your journey around them rather than rushing through.

## What type of car should you rent for the Makkah-Madinah journey?

**The short answer:** a mid-size sedan or compact SUV is the right choice for most pilgrim families. Sedans handle the highway comfortably, and SUVs give you extra luggage space if you are carrying bags for the full pilgrimage. Avoid compact economy cars if you are travelling with three or more passengers plus luggage.

The road itself does not require a 4x4 or anything rugged. Highway 15 is paved and well-maintained throughout. The SUV choice is about comfort and cargo, not terrain.

Consider these options:

**Economy sedan (Toyota Yaris or equivalent):** Suits a solo traveller or couple with light bags. Fuel-efficient and easy to park in city limits. From SAR 120 per day.

**Mid-size sedan (Toyota Corolla, Hyundai Elantra, or equivalent):** The most popular choice for pilgrim families. Comfortable on long highways, adequate boot space for two to three bags. From SAR 160 per day.

**Compact SUV (Toyota RAV4, Hyundai Tucson, or equivalent):** Best for families of four to five, or anyone carrying Zamzam water cans home. The higher seating position is also more comfortable for older travellers. From SAR 240 per day.

**7-seat MPV or minivan:** Available for larger family groups. Book early, as these are in limited supply during peak Umrah season. From SAR 320 per day.

Air conditioning is standard in every rental vehicle. Do not even think about driving this route without it. Temperatures in the Hejaz region regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius from April through October.

## Tips for driving in Makkah and Madinah city limits

City driving in both holy cities has its own rhythm, and it is worth knowing before you arrive.

**In Makkah:** The area immediately surrounding Masjid Al-Haram is pedestrian-heavy and congested at all prayer times. The Haram itself is off-limits to non-Muslims, and checkpoint roads enforce this. If you are a non-Muslim driving through the region (accompanying a pilgrim group or as support staff), be aware that certain roads near the Haram perimeter are restricted.

For Muslim pilgrims, parking near the Haram is limited. The best strategy is to stay at one of the [hotels near Masjid Al-Haram](/hotels/makkah/masjid-al-haram) that offer parking facilities, or use the official multi-storey car parks on the outskirts and walk or take the shuttle.

Al-Haram zone traffic management intensifies dramatically in the final ten days of Ramadan and during Hajj. If you are arriving during these periods with a rental car, plan your parking strategy before you drive into the city centre.

**In Madinah:** The area around Masjid Al-Nabawi is similarly pedestrianised close to the mosque. The city is, however, generally easier to navigate than Makkah. Roads are wider in the newer districts, and parking is more available near accommodation. The [hotels near Masjid Al-Nabawi in Madinah](/hotels/madinah) closest to the mosque are within walking distance, which means you can park once and leave the car until departure.

**General Saudi driving rules to know:**

- Seatbelts are mandatory for all passengers, front and back. Fines apply.
- Mobile phone use while driving is illegal and camera-enforced.
- Right-hand traffic applies throughout Saudi Arabia.
- Women have held full driving rights in Saudi Arabia since 2018.
- Overtaking on the right is technically prohibited but common. Stay alert.

Saudi roads use kilometre measurements. Speed signs are in km/h. Most rental GPS systems can be set to English and will use the correct units.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can non-Muslims rent a car to drive on Highway 15 between Makkah and Madinah?

Yes. Non-Muslims can legally rent a car and drive on Highway 15. The road itself has no religious restrictions. However, non-Muslims cannot enter the city of Makkah. Road checkpoints on routes into Makkah ask for confirmation of Muslim faith, and the city is off-limits to non-Muslim visitors. Non-Muslims can drive to Madinah without restriction.

### Is it safe to drive from Makkah to Madinah at night?

The drive is generally safe at night outside of peak congestion periods. Highway 15 is lit in populated sections and patrolled by traffic police. The main risks are fatigue on a 420-kilometre route and reduced visibility in unfamiliar road sections. If you plan a night drive, take a rest stop halfway and make sure all passengers are wearing seatbelts. Avoid night driving during the final days of Ramadan when road traffic increases sharply.

### How long does the drive take during Hajj season?

During peak Hajj days (8th to 13th of Dhul Hijjah), the Saudi government implements strict traffic management on roads into Makkah. Self-drive travel during this period is heavily restricted, and what is normally a four-to-five-hour journey can become an eight-plus-hour ordeal or may be blocked entirely. Pilgrims performing Hajj are strongly advised to use official Hajj bus services during these specific days. Outside of those dates, normal travel times apply.

### Can I pick up a rental car at Jeddah airport and drop it off in Madinah?

Yes. One-way rentals between Jeddah (JED), Makkah, and Madinah are available through UTUBooking. A one-way drop-off fee applies and is shown clearly during the booking process before you confirm payment. This arrangement is popular with pilgrims who fly into Jeddah, complete Umrah in Makkah, then travel to Madinah before flying home from Prince Mohammad Bin Abdulaziz International Airport (MED).

### What happens if I get a speeding fine in Saudi Arabia with a rental car?

Saudi traffic fines are issued to the registered vehicle owner, which in this case is the rental company. The company will charge the fine amount, plus an administration fee, to the credit card used for the rental. The administration fee varies by company but typically ranges from SAR 50 to SAR 150 per fine. Speed cameras on Highway 15 are frequent and accurate. Staying at or below the posted limit is the simplest way to avoid this cost.
