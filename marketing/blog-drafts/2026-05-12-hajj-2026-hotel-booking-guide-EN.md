## When Does Hajj 2026 Take Place and When Does Hotel Inventory Open?

**The short answer:** Hajj 2026 falls on [5 to 10 Dhul Hijjah 1447 AH](https://www.islamicfinder.org/islamic-calendar/), corresponding approximately to late May 2026. The peak hotel demand window runs from late May through early June. Inventory for those dates began releasing to tour operators in late 2025 and will thin out significantly by March 2026.

Dhul Hijjah is the twelfth and final month of the Islamic calendar. The Hajj rituals, Arafat, Muzdalifah, Mina, and Tawaf Al-Ifadha, are concentrated in the first ten days of that month. Every pilgrim in the world needs accommodation in Makkah during exactly the same window.

The [Saudi Ministry of Hajj and Umrah](https://www.haj.gov.sa/en/InternalPages/Pages/About) releases hotel allocation to licensed operators on a rolling basis starting around 18 months before the season. By 12 months out, the best Zone 1 properties are typically already allocated. By 6 months out, mid-range Zone 1 rooms are going fast. By 3 months out, you are booking what is left.

The implication is straightforward. Booking in spring 2026 for a late May 2026 Hajj gives you first access to available inventory at pre-peak rates. Waiting until Ramadan 2026 puts you last in a very long queue.

The next section explains exactly why prices move so sharply once Dhul Hijjah approaches.

## Why Do Hajj Hotel Prices Triple During Dhul Hijjah?

**The short answer:** Makkah has a fixed supply of hotel rooms. Approximately [1.8 million international pilgrims](https://www.haj.gov.sa/en/InternalPages/Pages/About) travel to Makkah for Hajj annually, all requiring accommodation in the same 10-day window. When demand is fixed and supply is finite, prices spike. That is not price gouging. It is basic supply and demand in one of the most constrained markets on earth.

Here's the reality: there are roughly 100,000 hotel rooms within 2 kilometres of Masjid Al-Haram. During Hajj, every single one is occupied. The Saudi government controls new hotel development in the Haram expansion zone, which means supply growth is slow while global Muslim population growth continues.

The [NUSUK platform](https://www.nusuk.sa/en), the Saudi government's official Hajj management system, coordinates pilgrim movement and accommodation allocation. Licensed operators receive their allocations early. Independent travelers who did not secure accommodation through an operator before the season face a market with almost no remaining supply and extreme pricing pressure.

Room rates in Zone 1 follow a predictable curve. Off-season (January to April) rates run SAR 400 to SAR 900 per night. As Dhul Hijjah approaches, those same rooms reach SAR 2,000 to SAR 4,000 per night. That is not an accident of timing. It reflects real scarcity.

Booking early means you pay the off-peak rate for on-peak dates. That difference alone can save a family of four SAR 8,000 to SAR 20,000 over a 10-day stay.

The zone you choose determines both your price and your experience. That decision is covered next.

## Zone 1 vs Zone 2: Which Makkah Accommodation Zone Is Right for You?

Most guides describe Zone 1 and Zone 2 by star ratings and price brackets. That is only part of the story. The more important factor is walking time to Masjid Al-Haram, because during Hajj, you will make that walk many times each day.

**Zone 1** covers the area within approximately 500 to 800 metres of the Masjid Al-Haram perimeter. Hotels here include properties directly in the Abraj Al-Bait towers complex, facing the Haram, as well as mid-range hotels on the streets immediately surrounding the mosque. Walking time from Zone 1 ranges from 2 to 15 minutes depending on the specific property. Rates during Hajj season run from SAR 1,800 to SAR 4,500 per night.

**Zone 2** covers the area from approximately 800 metres to 2 kilometres from Masjid Al-Haram, including districts like Ajyad, Jumeirah, and parts of Al-Mashaer. Walking time from Zone 2 ranges from 15 to 40 minutes. Many Zone 2 hotels operate shuttle services to the Haram gates during Hajj. Rates during Hajj season run from SAR 800 to SAR 2,000 per night.

Look, the right choice depends on two things: budget and physical capability.

For elderly pilgrims, pilgrims with mobility limitations, or families with young children, Zone 1 proximity is not a luxury. It is a practical necessity. That SAR 700-per-night price premium disappears when you consider the alternative is a 35-minute walk at 3 a.m. for Fajr in June heat.

## How Long Do Hajj Pilgrims Typically Stay in Makkah?

**The short answer:** Most Hajj itineraries allocate 5 to 15 nights in Makkah. The 5-day minimum covers the core Hajj rituals from 8 Dhul Hijjah through 12 or 13 Dhul Hijjah. Extended stays of 10 to 15 nights allow time for additional worship before and after the main rituals, as well as the Madinah portion of the pilgrimage.

The standard Hajj package structure runs as follows:

- **Short Hajj package (5-7 nights Makkah):** Covers the core ritual days only. Travel in, perform Hajj, travel out. Typically used by pilgrims on tighter budgets or with time constraints.
- **Standard Hajj package (10-12 nights Makkah):** The most common structure. Includes 3-5 nights before the main Hajj rituals for acclimatisation and additional worship, then 7 nights through completion.
- **Full pilgrimage package (12-15 nights Makkah + 7-8 nights Madinah):** The complete Hajj and Madinah combination. Pilgrims visit both holy cities, either Makkah-first or Madinah-first depending on operator structure.

## How Do National Hajj Quotas Affect Hotel Availability?

**The short answer:** Each country receives a Hajj quota set by the Saudi government, typically calculated at [approximately 1,000 pilgrims per 1 million Muslims](https://www.haj.gov.sa/en/InternalPages/Pages/About) in the population. These quotas determine how many citizens from each country can perform Hajj in a given year, and they directly affect when hotel inventory is allocated and when demand spikes in each market.

Here is how this plays out in the major sending countries:

**Indonesia** receives the largest global Hajj quota, with over 221,000 places in recent years. [Indonesian national quota coordination](https://kemenag.go.id/) runs through the Ministry of Religious Affairs. Waiting lists run 20 to 40 years in some provinces. Operators allocate hotel blocks very early, often 18 to 24 months in advance.

**Pakistan** receives approximately 179,000 places. The [Pakistan Hajj scheme](https://hajj.com.pk/) operates through the Ministry of Religious Affairs, with separate government and private operator streams. Private operator block bookings drive early hotel demand from Pakistan from around October the year before.

**Turkey** receives approximately 79,000 places, coordinated through the [Diyanet Foundation](https://www.diyanet.gov.tr/en-EN). Turkish operators have historically been aggressive early-season hotel block buyers. Their procurement drives Zone 1 scarcity earlier than many other markets.

**Malaysia** receives approximately 31,500 places through [Tabung Haji](https://www.tabunghaji.gov.my/), which manages both the savings scheme and the group travel logistics. Tabung Haji's large group bookings are placed years in advance.

**India** receives approximately 175,000 places through the [Haj Committee of India](https://www.hajcommittee.gov.in/). Demand is growing steadily, with the Indian private operator sector adding significant hotel block pressure alongside government scheme bookings.

The practical implication for individual pilgrims: if your nationality is from any of these high-quota countries, your national operators are already purchasing hotel blocks. Pilgrims who book through packages before those blocks are fully allocated get the best rooms at the lowest prices. Pilgrims who book after the block release is complete pay spot rates for whatever remains.
