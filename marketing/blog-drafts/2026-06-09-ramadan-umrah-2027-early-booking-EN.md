## When Is Ramadan 2027 and Why Does It Matter for Booking?

**The short answer:** Ramadan 2027 falls approximately from 20 January 2027 to 18 February 2027, based on the Islamic lunar calendar. The final ten nights, beginning around 8 February 2027, are the peak demand window. Ramadan moves approximately eleven days earlier each Gregorian year. In 2027, it falls in January and February, which means cooler weather than recent Ramadan seasons. That is a significant draw for pilgrims who found previous Ramadans during summer months difficult. Cooler temperatures increase demand. More pilgrims plan to come. Fewer rooms remain.

Tour operators across Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Jordan, Pakistan, Malaysia, and Indonesia block-book hotel inventories for Ramadan packages up to twelve months in advance. ## Which Hotels Near Masjid Al-Haram Fill Up Fastest During Ramadan?

**The short answer:** Hotels within 300 metres of Masjid Al-Haram fill first, typically 9 to 12 months before Ramadan. These include the Abraj Al-Bait towers (Fairmont, Pullman, Mövenpick, Swissôtel), Hilton Makkah Convention, and Millennium Makkah Al Naseem. Mid-range properties at 300 to 600 metres fill next, followed by Zone 2 hotels at 600 metres to 2 kilometres.

Let's be honest about what proximity means for Ramadan Umrah.

You will be making Tarawih prayer every night. You may be going for Tahajjud in the last ten nights. You may want to catch Fajr without navigating a 35-minute walk at 4 a.m. in January. The difference between a hotel 200 metres from the nearest Haram gate and a hotel 900 metres away is not a preference. For many pilgrims, it determines the shape of the entire experience.

**Zone 1 properties (under 500 metres):** These are the properties in and immediately around the Abraj Al-Bait complex and the streets directly surrounding Masjid Al-Haram. Walking times range from 2 to 12 minutes to the nearest gate. Rates during Ramadan run from SAR 950 to SAR 4,500 per night depending on star rating, view, and room type. Five-star properties with Kaaba views reach SAR 3,500 to SAR 5,500 per night in the final ten nights.

**Mid-range Zone 1 (200 to 500 metres):** Properties like Dar Al Tawhid, Al Haritha Hotel, and several three-star options in the Al Ajyad corridor. These are the most competitive segment during Ramadan. They offer genuine proximity at prices SAR 600 to SAR 1,600 per night, and they fill faster than any other category.

**Zone 2 (500 metres to 2 kilometres):** Hotels in Aziziyah and the surrounding districts. Walking time ranges from 20 to 45 minutes. Many operate shuttle buses during Ramadan. Rates run from SAR 450 to SAR 1,200 per night. These are a genuine option for pilgrims who want to manage costs without sacrificing a good Makkah stay.

## How Much More Expensive Are Makkah Hotels During Ramadan?

**The short answer:** Expect Makkah hotel prices to be 2 to 4 times higher during Ramadan than during off-season months. A Zone 1 room that costs SAR 500 per night in October becomes SAR 1,400 to SAR 2,200 per night during the first three weeks of Ramadan, and SAR 2,500 to SAR 5,000 per night in the last ten nights.

The pricing curve follows a predictable pattern, and understanding it helps you time your booking.

Off-season rates (March to October, excluding Hajj): Zone 1 hotels run from SAR 350 to SAR 900 per night. Zone 2 from SAR 200 to SAR 500 per night. These are the rates you will see if you book a Ramadan Umrah hotel today, using January 2027 dates.

Early Ramadan (nights 1 to 20): Prices are elevated but not at peak. Zone 1 mid-range properties run from SAR 1,200 to SAR 2,200 per night. Five-star Zone 1 from SAR 2,500 to SAR 4,000 per night.

Last ten nights (nights 21 to 30): The sharpest price increases in the Makkah hotel calendar, outside of the Hajj season itself. Zone 1 mid-range climbs to SAR 2,000 to SAR 4,500 per night. Five-star Kaaba-view rooms routinely reach SAR 5,000 to SAR 7,000 per night. Some properties apply minimum stay requirements of 5 to 7 nights for these dates.

The insight that most travelers miss: booking six months out locks in rates that are often 40 to 60 percent below what the same room will cost in November or December 2026, even though Ramadan is still two months away. Hotels price on demand signals. Demand signals for Ramadan rooms peak in late autumn as the season approaches. Booking now captures the pre-peak rate.

## How Do You Book Ramadan Umrah Flights Early?

**The short answer:** Book Ramadan Umrah flights 6 to 9 months before travel. For Ramadan 2027, that means booking between June and October 2026. GCC-based carriers including Saudia, Flynas, Flydubai, Air Arabia, and Jazeera Airways serve the main Ramadan corridors. Lock your flights after securing your hotel dates, not before.

Here is why hotel-first, flight-second is the right sequence.

Your hotel dates determine your flight dates. Booking a flight before confirming accommodation leaves you with a firm travel window but uncertain accommodation, which is a problem when Makkah hotels during the final ten nights sometimes require minimum-stay commitments.

**Airport choice:** Jeddah King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED) is the primary entry point for Makkah-bound pilgrims. Ground transport from Jeddah to Makkah takes approximately 90 minutes. If your itinerary begins in Madinah, Madinah Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz Airport (MED) is the closer option. Check which airlines serve MED from your departure city before choosing this route.

**Fare timing:** GCC airlines typically release Ramadan fares 6 to 10 months in advance, which aligns with the hotel booking window. Booking both together in the same planning session is the most efficient approach.

**Price movement:** Ramadan airfares follow the same demand curve as hotels. Fares on high-demand routes (Riyadh-JED, Dubai-JED, Kuwait-JED) increase by 30 to 80 percent in the months immediately before Ramadan as remaining inventory tightens. Locking fares in August or September 2026 for January 2027 travel is consistently the better financial decision.

## What Should Your Ramadan Umrah Checklist Include?

**The short answer:** A complete Ramadan Umrah checklist covers documentation, health, packing, worship preparation, and travel logistics. Most pilgrims underestimate the practical preparation required. Starting this list six months out prevents last-minute problems.

Here are the eight items that matter most.

**1. Umrah visa confirmation.** Confirm that your Umrah visa category and timing are correct for Ramadan travel. GCC nationals check their entry requirements. Non-GCC nationals confirm the Umrah visa window for their nationality.

**2. Hotel booking with proximity data.** Confirm your hotel is within your target walking distance of Masjid Al-Haram. Use UTUBooking's proximity filter to verify actual walking time from the hotel lobby to the nearest Haram gate.

**3. Flight booking with arrival buffer.** Arrive at least one day before your intended first Tarawih prayer. Long flights followed immediately by Haram visits are physically demanding. Build in recovery time.

**4. Health preparation.** Consult a doctor if you have any chronic conditions. Crowd management in Masjid Al-Haram during Ramadan requires physical readiness. Ensure any required vaccinations are current.

**5. Ihram garments and essentials.** Pack two sets of Ihram for men. Women should have modest, comfortable clothing suitable for extended periods of worship. Light layers work well for January and February in Makkah.

**6. Worship schedule planning.** Know the Tarawih prayer times for your planned stay. Plan which nights you want to be in Masjid Al-Haram for extended worship. The last ten nights require advance planning around crowds.

**7. Emergency contacts and travel insurance.** Confirm your travel insurance covers medical evacuation. Keep a note of the Saudi emergency number (911) and the nearest hospital to your hotel.

**8. SAR cash and digital payments.** Most Makkah businesses accept card payment, but having SAR 500 to SAR 1,000 in cash is advisable for markets, smaller restaurants, and tips. UTUBooking confirms bookings in SAR so there are no currency conversion surprises at check-in.
