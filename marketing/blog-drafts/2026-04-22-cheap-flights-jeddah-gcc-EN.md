## What Is the Cheapest Time to Fly from GCC Cities to Jeddah?

The cheapest time to fly from GCC cities to Jeddah is outside the peak Umrah windows: avoid mid-Ramadan (last 10 nights), the weeks immediately before Hajj, and school holidays. Flying on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Saturday mornings typically yields the lowest fares, with savings of 20 to 40 percent compared to Thursday and Friday departures.

The pattern holds across all five major GCC departure cities. Ramadan brings a surge in Umrah intention, and airlines price accordingly. The [International Air Transport Association](https://www.iata.org/en/publications/economics/air-travel-demand/) consistently shows Middle East intra-regional routes spike hardest during religious-season demand peaks.

Here's what the seasonal split looks like in practice:

**Low season (best fares):** January to February before Ramadan, June through September (excluding Hajj weeks), and late October to mid-November.

**Shoulder season (moderate fares):** Early Ramadan (first two weeks), late Dhul Qa'dah, and February school holiday weeks.

**Peak season (highest fares):** Last 10 nights of Ramadan, Dhul Hijjah (Hajj season), and the Eid al-Adha travel window.

For 2026, Ramadan begins around late February. Budget your travel accordingly, and you will find Jeddah fares are far more reasonable in the earlier weeks of the month.

## How Far Ahead Should You Book GCC-to-Jeddah Flights for Umrah?

Book GCC-to-Jeddah flights 8 to 12 weeks before your departure date for the best combination of seat availability and fare pricing. Booking earlier than 12 weeks risks paying inflated speculative fares. Booking later than 6 weeks in Umrah season pushes you into scarcity pricing, especially on Saudia and flydubai routes.

The 8-to-12-week window is the sweet spot confirmed by revenue management data from [OAG Aviation Worldwide](https://www.oag.com/). Within that window, airlines have filled enough seats to lower risk, but have not yet switched to last-minute yield-maximisation pricing.

A few practical notes on this:

If you are travelling from **Riyadh (RUH)**, the route is domestic, so different pricing rules apply. Saudia and flynas both release domestic fares in cycles, and you can often find strong deals at the 6-week mark as well.

If you are travelling from **Dubai (DXB)**, flydubai has become the dominant budget option, but Emirates codeshares can undercut on certain booking classes. Check both under the same search.

If you are travelling from **Kuwait (KWI), Doha (DOH), or Bahrain (BAH)**, the market is thinner. Fewer flights per day means prices tighten faster. For these routes, lean toward the 10-to-12-week window rather than the 8-week end.

The best strategy: set a fare alert at 12 weeks out, then book when the price hits your target or at 8 weeks, whichever comes first.

## When Do Jeddah Flight Prices Spike? The Hajj Season Calendar

Jeddah flight prices spike hardest in the three weeks before Hajj (Dhul Hijjah 1 to 8) and during the last 10 nights of Ramadan. In 2026, expect a secondary spike around Eid al-Fitr departures. Prices can increase 60 to 150 percent above baseline during these windows. Saudi authorities also restrict which passport holders can enter Makkah outside formal Hajj permits, which concentrates all demand into a narrow window.

Here is the 2026 calendar to watch:

**Ramadan 2026 (approximate):** Late February to late March. The last 10 nights, Laylat al-Qadr through Eid al-Fitr, drive the sharpest demand. Expect fares to peak in the final week of Ramadan.

**Eid al-Fitr 2026:** Late March. The outbound surge from Jeddah begins here as pilgrims return. Inbound fares to Jeddah ease slightly after Eid, making this one of the better short windows to travel affordably.

**Dhul Qa'dah 2026:** Fares in this month are typically shoulder-level. It is an underrated time for Umrah, with crowds lower and costs down.

**Hajj 2026 (Dhul Hijjah):** Estimated late May to early June. Fares to Jeddah spike from around Dhul Hijjah 1 (approximately May 18, 2026). Unless you hold a valid Hajj permit, you cannot enter Makkah during this period. Avoid booking Jeddah arrivals in this window unless your permit is confirmed.

**Post-Hajj window:** Mid-June onward. One of the cheapest Jeddah fare windows of the year. Hotels near Masjid Al-Haram also drop significantly. For independent Umrah travellers, this is a genuinely undervalued travel period.

Plan your Umrah around these windows and the fare savings can be substantial.

## What to Do After You Land at Jeddah King Abdulaziz Airport

After landing at King Abdulaziz International Airport (IATA: JED), pilgrims have three immediate priorities: transportation to Makkah or Madinah, accommodation check-in, and, for those not yet in a state of Ihram, preparing for Umrah rituals at the Miqat.

King Abdulaziz International Airport sits approximately 90 kilometres north of Makkah. It has a dedicated Hajj Terminal, though Umrah pilgrims typically arrive at the main terminals (Terminal 1 for Saudia domestic, Terminal 2 for international carriers). Travel time to Makkah by road is 60 to 90 minutes depending on traffic.

Your main ground transport options from the airport:

**Private transfer:** The most straightforward option for pilgrims arriving with luggage and a group. Book in advance through your Umrah operator or through the UTUBooking [car rental from Jeddah airport](https://utubooking.com/car-rental/saudi-arabia) service.

**SAPTCO bus:** Saudi Arabia's public bus operator runs scheduled services between the airport and Makkah. Fares are low (under SAR 30 as of early 2026), but timing depends on the schedule and buses fill quickly during peak Umrah arrivals.

**Shared taxi (hailing):** Available outside the arrivals hall. Negotiate the fare before departure. Typically SAR 80 to SAR 150 per vehicle to central Makkah.

For accommodation, the area around Masjid Al-Haram books up fast in Umrah season. Review [hotels near Masjid Al-Haram](https://utubooking.com/hotels/makkah/masjid-al-haram) before your flight, and secure your rooms at the same time as your tickets. Availability near the Grand Mosque shrinks rapidly in the 4-to-6-week window before travel.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the cheapest GCC city to fly to Jeddah from?**

Riyadh is typically the lowest-cost departure point because the Riyadh-Jeddah route is domestic within Saudi Arabia, attracting the highest competition and the lowest base fares. International routes from Dubai run a close second thanks to the volume of flights and the presence of budget carriers like flydubai and Air Arabia.

**How much does a GCC to Jeddah flight cost in 2026?**

Economy fares in shoulder season range from approximately SAR 180 (Riyadh domestic) to SAR 900 (Kuwait or Doha during Ramadan peaks). Most international GCC-to-Jeddah routes in low season fall between SAR 300 and SAR 600 return. Peak Umrah and Hajj windows push prices significantly higher.

**Is it cheaper to fly into Jeddah or Madinah for Umrah?**

Jeddah is the main international gateway and has far more flights, which drives fares down through competition. Madinah (MED) has fewer international frequencies from GCC cities, so it typically costs more per seat despite the shorter ground distance to the holy sites for pilgrims visiting both cities. Most pilgrims fly into Jeddah and arrange ground transport to Makkah and Madinah separately.

**Do Jeddah flights get cheaper last minute during Umrah season?**

No. Unlike leisure routes, Jeddah-bound flights during Umrah season almost never get cheaper last minute. Demand is religiously motivated and largely inelastic, which means airlines have no incentive to discount unsold seats close to departure. Last-minute bookings during Ramadan or Dhul Hijjah routinely cost 60 to 100 percent more than advance fares.
